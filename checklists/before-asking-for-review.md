# Checklist: Before Asking For Review

Use this before sending the repo, a review packet, or a feedback ask to someone else.

The point is to get one useful note without turning the ask into spam or pulling private context into public work.

## Fit

- The person or group already cares about coding-agent workflow, evals, guardrails, or safer contribution patterns.
- The ask is relevant to the place you are sending it.
- The ask is going to one person or one small group, not a broad list.
- You are not reposting because an earlier post is pending, missing, or quiet.

## Ask

- Ask for one missing failure mode, unclear checklist line, weak stop condition, or overconfident claim.
- Do not ask for stars, follows, shares, adoption claims, or private stories.
- Link to `docs/review-packet.md` or `docs/reviewer-brief.md`.
- Include one public-safe feedback path if useful.

## Safety

- No private logs.
- No secrets or keys.
- No customer or user details.
- No private repo names.
- No account, billing, wallet, session, or auth details.
- No screenshots with private context.
- No raw assistant transcripts.

If the real story matters, rewrite it as a fake scenario first.

## Stop

Stop before sending if:

- the target person or channel is not explicit
- the community rules are unknown
- the ask would be a cold DM to a stranger
- the same link was already posted recently
- the wording depends on private context
- the post makes the repo sound more adopted, tested, or complete than it is

## Good Enough To Send

```text
Target:
Why this person/place fits:
One thing asked for:
Public-safe link:
Private details removed: yes/no
Approval to send: yes/no
```
