# Checklist: Before Claiming Traction

Use this before writing that people are reviewing, adopting, using, sharing, or interested in a repo.

The point is simple: say what is visible. Do not dress up silence as momentum.

## Public Signals

Check only public or approved sources.

- Stars:
- Forks:
- Open issues:
- Pull requests:
- Public comments:
- Accepted posts:
- Direct feedback you have permission to mention:

If the answer is zero, say zero or say nothing.

## Wording

Use boring facts:

```text
No public feedback yet. Next step is one focused review ask after approval.
```

Not this:

```text
People are starting to look at it.
```

Unless people are visibly starting to look at it.

## Stop

Stop before writing traction claims if:

- the only signal is a pending or moderated post
- the account or post status recently changed
- the repo has no public issues, comments, forks, or stars
- feedback came through a private channel
- the wording makes the project sound more adopted, reviewed, or complete than it is
- the next step is a post, DM, issue, form, or account change that needs approval

## Good Enough

```text
Visible public signals:
Private or unmentionable signals removed:
Claim being made:
Evidence for that claim:
Needs approval before posting: yes/no
```

If the evidence feels thin, keep the update internal.
